# Stargazing Knowledge Guide

## City Lights Index / Bortle Scale

The city lights index represents how much light pollution is expected at a location. It is based on the idea of the Bortle scale, where lower values mean darker natural skies and higher values mean brighter urban skies.

A value near 1 means the sky is very dark and suitable for faint deep-sky objects such as galaxies, nebulae, and the Milky Way. A value near 9 means the sky is heavily light-polluted, usually in a dense urban area, where only bright stars, the Moon, and planets are easy to observe.

## Cloud Cover

Cloud cover is one of the most important limiting factors for stargazing. Even if the sky is dark, heavy cloud cover can block stars and deep-sky objects. Low cloud cover is usually better.

## Transparency

Transparency describes how clear the atmosphere is. High transparency means less haze, smoke, humidity, or dust. It is especially important for observing faint objects such as nebulae, galaxies, and the Milky Way.

## Seeing

Seeing describes atmospheric steadiness. Good seeing makes stars appear more stable and sharp. Seeing is especially important for observing the Moon, planets, and double stars at higher magnification.

## Moon Illumination

A bright Moon can wash out faint stars and deep-sky objects. Low moon illumination is usually better for dark-sky observing. However, a bright Moon can still be good for lunar observation.

## What to Observe

If the sky is dark, transparent, and has low moon interference, it is suitable for deep-sky objects such as galaxies, nebulae, star clusters, and the Milky Way.

If the Moon is bright but seeing is stable, it may still be a good night for observing the Moon or bright planets.

If cloud cover is high, stargazing quality is usually poor regardless of other factors.

## Data Source Limitation

Astrospheric is preferred because it provides astronomy-specific sky quality estimates such as seeing and transparency. Open-Meteo is useful as a fallback source, but it does not directly provide astronomy-specific seeing and transparency, so these values may need to be approximated.
